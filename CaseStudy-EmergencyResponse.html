Emergency Flood Response Rapid Deploy

4-Hour Setup, 2,000 People Served: Rapid-Deploy Water Response to the 2025 Manitoba Spring Floods

RR
Aarvish Rapid-Response Operations
Activated by Manitoba Emergency Measures Organization · Interlake Region · April–May 2025
Executive Summary

Between April 18 and May 27, 2025, an unusually rapid spring snowmelt combined with frozen subsurface saturated the Red and Assiniboine river basins, triggering the most significant Manitoba spring flooding event in over a decade. Water-treatment intakes at three Interlake-region municipalities were compromised, and 2,047 residents were evacuated to three temporary reception centres.

Aarvish was activated by the Manitoba Emergency Measures Organization (EMO) at 06:14 on April 19. The first containerized AWG-RO Rapid-Deploy unit was producing potable water at the Lundar evacuation centre within 4 hours of arrival on site. Across 39 days of continuous operation across three sites, the system delivered 1.78 million litres of WHO-grade drinking water with zero waterborne-illness incidents reported by the on-site medical command.

01The 2025 Manitoba Spring Flood Event

The 2025 spring melt was characterized by what hydrologists at Manitoba Hydrology & Forecasting Centre called a "perfect frozen-ground sequence":

  • Above-average snowpack: 142% of normal across the Red River watershed (Snow Water Equivalent measurements, March 2025).
  • Late, rapid thaw: Daily mean temperatures jumped from −8°C to +14°C within 8 days starting April 12 — vs. a normal 21-day transition.
  • Saturated frozen subsurface: The previous fall's heavy precipitation left soils unable to absorb meltwater. Surface runoff coefficients reached 0.89 in some Interlake catchments.

Three municipal water treatment plants in the Interlake region — Lundar, Eriksdale, and Ashern — saw their surface-water intakes compromised by elevated turbidity, Total Organic Carbon, and bacterial loading from upstream agricultural runoff. By April 17, all three had issued precautionary boil-water advisories, and by April 18 Manitoba EMO had begun population evacuation to designated reception centres.

"We needed clean water at three sites simultaneously, with zero infrastructure on the ground, within hours not days. This is exactly the scenario the centralized model cannot solve." — Manitoba EMO Operations Coordinator (situation report, April 19, 2025)

03Rapid-Deploy Architecture

The Aarvish Rapid-Deploy unit is a purpose-built variant optimized for emergency conditions. The core differences from the standard community AWG-RO platform:

  • 20-foot trailer-mounted form factor. Towed by any standard pickup truck. Self-deploying outriggers; no crane required.
  • Aggressive pre-treatment train. Sand + multi-media + bag pre-filter rated for source turbidity up to 200 NTU (vs. standard 5 NTU).
  • Surge buffer. 5,000 L distribution tank handles meal-hour demand peaks (3,500 L over 90 minutes during evening service).
  • Pre-tested deployment kits. Generator backup, UV verification kit, hose-couplings, food-grade tanks, signage — all in two pre-positioned containers.

What This Proves About Emergency Water Doctrine

Pre-positioned, modular, self-powered water production isn't just an alternative to bottled water trucking — it's a strictly superior emergency-response posture. Aarvish has since signed a 5-year Master Standing Offer with Manitoba EMO making the Rapid-Deploy fleet the primary water response asset for declared flooding and wildfire-evacuation events province-wide.

08Lessons Learned for Future Emergency Doctrine

  • Pre-positioning beats post-event procurement. Every minute saved in the activation chain comes from work done before the event. Aarvish keeps three units field-ready at the Winnipeg warehouse, fuelled, telemetry-tested, and pre-cleared for highway transport.
  • Standing MOUs eliminate political hesitation. The 06:14 activation call became the 06:42 dispatch because Manitoba EMO already had a signed framework agreement. No purchase orders, no meetings, no committee approvals.
  • On-board telemetry is a regulatory shortcut. Real-time water quality data uplinked to EMO's operations centre allowed health authorities to issue rapid clearance for human consumption. Without it, a 24-hour lab-confirmation delay would have applied.
  • The food-prep load is bigger than the drinking load. Reception centres use roughly 28% of treated water for mass meal preparation. Future units will be sized with that in mind.
  • Plastic-waste avoidance is a co-benefit with measurable value. 14.2 tonnes of plastic that did not enter the Manitoba landfill stream is a real environmental outcome that can be reported in Aarvish's ESG and ICIP grant submissions.
